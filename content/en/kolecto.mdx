## Context

Kolecto simplifies the administrative and financial daily life of small businesses — bank reconciliation, client/supplier invoice management, payments. A Crédit Agricole subsidiary, serving non-expert profiles: craftsmen, farmers, shopkeepers.

Ongoing mission via Thiga on the **Sales Squad** — the scope covering everything related to the commercial relationship: quotes, invoices, client directory, product catalog.

## The challenge

The existing billing module needed a **deep backend overhaul** to comply with the 2026 e-invoicing reform (Factur-X / UBL formats, submission via the French Public Invoicing Portal). A serious technical undertaking, with a non-negotiable regulatory deadline.

In parallel: a product differentiation opportunity. Kolecto wanted to reach user segments poorly served by generalist competitors (Pennylane, Indy, Evoliz...) — namely **agriculture** and **construction**, which have very specific regulatory and business needs.

## The key decision

<Callout title="Differentiating positioning">
Rather than copying generalist competitors, I structured discovery around the sectors where Kolecto can win: agriculture and construction. These sectors have specific needs — progress billing, regulated deposits, sector-specific mandatory mentions, VAT on margin — that generalists handle poorly and specialists handle too narrowly.

The roadmap was built on this double logic: e-invoicing compliance on one side, affinity differentiation on the other.
</Callout>

## What I did

<Callout title="Field instinct meets regulation">
Building a billing module compliant with the 2026 e-invoicing reform meant handling gnarly edge cases, like supporting the "Ticket Z" end-of-day cash report. Instead of running weeks of academic discovery, I fell back on my old waiter and bartender jobs from my student years, plus my time at Pyxo working with restaurant owners. I already knew how a till closes out at the end of a shift — that saved weeks of interviews to learn constraints I already understood.
</Callout>

**Backend overhaul of the Quotes & Billing module** — not built from scratch, but a deep overhaul for regulatory compliance: adapting to e-invoice formats (Factur-X, UBL), integration with the Public Invoicing Portal, anticipating 2026 obligations. UX designed for non-experts with no accounting background.

**Sector discovery — agriculture & construction** — in-depth business interviews, mapping of regulatory specifics (mandatory mentions, progress billing situations, VAT on margin...), identifying differentiation opportunities vs. generalist and niche competitors.

**Affinity roadmap** — structuring a "Kolecto for construction pros" and "Kolecto for farmers" offering: specific features, adapted onboarding flows, prioritization against niche competitors.

## What I learned

A regulatory compliance project can look purely technical. But when the whole market is forced to overhaul its backend at the same time, it's also a window to get ahead on differentiation — if you decide to use it.

At Kolecto, combining e-invoicing compliance with affinity discovery on agriculture and construction turned a forced constraint into a product choice.
