## Context

Calldesk automates repetitive phone calls via callbots able to resolve requests autonomously, 24/7. Low-code B2B SaaS, sold to companies handling large call volumes — telecom operators, insurers, public services.

2019-2021 — before ChatGPT, before conversational AI went mainstream. NLP was still a specialist's affair.

## The challenge

Time-to-production for a new client was **6 months**. For each onboarding: 2 developers + 1 CSM mobilized for weeks, largely manual work, no repeatability. Under these conditions, growing without exploding operational costs was impossible.

## The key decision

<Callout title="Diagnosis">
Rather than adding resources (hiring devs), I dug into the root causes of the slowness. The real problem: the platform required technical expertise to configure bots. CSMs depended entirely on devs for every setting — even the simplest ones.

I redesigned the product architecture so CSMs could **configure 80% of bots themselves**, without dev intervention.
</Callout>

The answer wasn't "more resources" but "a better product." That's a distinction that changes everything in B2B SaaS.

## What I did

**Call center diagnostic** — built a diagnostic tool to immerse in a prospect's call center operations: call flow analysis, identifying automatable use cases, mapping pain points. The diagnostic led to a calibrated bot proposal and an automation roadmap directly aligned with the call center's strategy. This is what made the sales pitch credible and differentiating.

**50+ user interviews** — CSMs, clients, integrators. Mapping every step of the configuration workflow, identifying where CSMs got stuck and called a dev, understanding why.

**Product architecture overhaul** — no-code UX oriented for CSMs, clean separation between business configuration (CSM autonomy) and technical setup (devs only for complex cases).

**Short-cycle delivery** — fast iterations with the team, field validation at every cycle with CSMs.

**Hybrid PM/pre-sales role** — supporting Sales on major account deals (CAC40, telecom operators). Technical product presentation, use case qualification, contribution to RFP responses.

## What I learned

The best feature is often the one that **removes a role from the workflow**, not the one that assists it.

And reducing time-to-market for a B2B SaaS is one of the most impactful product decisions you can make — because it directly changes the company's sales capacity, without hiring.
