## Context

Ledger Live is Ledger's crypto wallet application — millions of users, dozens of supported blockchains. The SWAP feature lets users exchange crypto directly in the app, without going through an external exchange.

A 4-month transition mission, while Ledger recruited its in-house PM. The product was mid-development with partner integrations stuck.

## The challenge

SWAP represents **70% of Ledger Live's revenue**. DEX integrations — including THORChain, which enables cross-chain swaps — had both technical and contractual blockers. The team was moving, but without clear product ownership for a few weeks.

## What I did

<Callout title="Week 1 priority">
Before touching anything, I spent the first 3 days interviewing each dev individually. Understanding who's blocking what, and why — technical vs. organizational — before making a single decision.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pragmatic trade-offs and technical trust">
Arriving in a complex context on the SWAP feature (generating 70% of the desktop app's exchange revenue), I had to unblock the integration of the decentralized THORChain protocol. Not claiming to be an absolute expert in cross-chain trading on arrival, I took a listening-first posture. I relied on the team's expert engineers to map out the blockers. I then formalized and proposed several integration scenarios with precise business and technical impacts, before delegating and fully trusting the technical team on operational execution to secure delivery within very tight deadlines.
</Callout>

**Full delivery ownership** — immediate operational ownership, clarifying priorities, unblocking contention points between the team and DEX partners.

**Partner coordination** — THORChain and other DEX integrations required intensive external coordination. Identifying real blockers — contractual vs. technical — and resolving both in parallel.

**Bridge feature discovery** — alongside SWAP delivery, 50+ user interviews to scope the next use case (Bridge = cross-chain transfer). Goal: leave a clean handoff for the next PM.

## What I learned

Delivery quality on a short mission depends on **how fast you acquire context**. Spending 3 days listening before acting can look slow. In practice, it saved me 3 weeks of bad decisions.

In a context where the previous PM has left and the team is moving without a clear direction, the first value you bring is stabilization — giving each dev a reliable point of contact who understands their work.
