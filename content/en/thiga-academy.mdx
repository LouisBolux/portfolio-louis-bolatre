## Context

Thiga Academy is Thiga's training arm — a Product Management consulting firm. It offers programs for in-house product teams: skill-building, discovery practices, Senior PM posture.

Since January 2025, I've been running training sessions for groups of Product Managers from different organizations.

## What I teach

**Product Discovery** — how to structure the exploration phase before specifying: user interviews, opportunities, prioritizing problems to solve. The goal: give PMs concrete tools so they don't jump to solutions too fast.

**Senior PM posture** — how to move from an executing PM to one who influences strategy. Stakeholder management, ownership, hard trade-offs, communicating upward.

**Concrete practices** — sessions rely on real cases and hands-on exercises rather than abstract theory. Participant feedback shapes the content.

## What I learned

Teaching forces you to formalize what you do intuitively. Explaining *why* you do things a certain way — not just *how* — has sharpened my own practice.

Mixed groups (PMs from very different sectors and companies) are also a useful signal source: what's universal in the craft, and what really depends on context.
